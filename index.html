++ Situation Report // Threat Assessment: Escalating ++

The Season of Fire has passed.
Nothing else has.

The Red Angel's Gate is sealed. The daemon tide is broken. The Imperium rebuilds, and its cathedrals declare the planet cleansed. Then the augurs light up: Wazdakka Gutsmek's Speedwaaagh! is inbound — the fastest Ork alive, at the head of a tide of engines — and the defence is not ready.

And against every record of his death, Commissar Yarrick has returned. The Old Man is back on Armageddon. Every faction in the sector is moving. Yours is one of them.

++ Rules of Engagement // What Your Warlord Signs ++

Built so everyone matters

Two armies, one loyalty

Run up to two Crusade forces in the same alliance. Field whichever fits the week — try the new codex without abandoning the war.

The third battle

Each phase, your second force earns a bonus game — against someone who's played less than the pack. The war pulls stragglers back in. That's the design.

Losing still counts

Every defeat banks alliance points, plus a Requisition Point and a Blessing for your next fight. Nobody walks away empty-handed.

No farming your mate

Full XP only the first time you fight each opponent per phase. Want glory? Fight the whole table.

No runaway legends

XP is capped per phase, and lagging forces get a boost. The gap stays a rivalry, never a chasm.

The warp is restless

Any battle may be declared Warp-Touched: anomalies each round, and things that hate both players. Killing them pays. Reasons classified.

Your story is the point

Named warlords, battle scars, relics, honours — recorded within 48 hours or it never happened. Fluff writers get famous at the epilogue.
